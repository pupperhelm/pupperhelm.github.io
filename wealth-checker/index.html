About the app

Wealth Checker Canada helps Canadians ages 25-49 answer a practical question: am I behind, on track or ahead for this stage of life?

Enter your age, province, household type, annual income and net worth. The app estimates your income and net worth percentile against public Canadian data, then shows how benchmark income and wealth change with age.

Use it when you are weighing home ownership, debt repayment, child-care costs, RRSP or TFSA saving, a career change, or simply want a clearer reference point. It is built for context, not a scorecard: comparisons are approximate and no two households are identical.

The benchmarks draw on Statistics Canada income, wealth and household data and are adjusted from published age groups, province and household type. The app does not require an account and processes your inputs on your device.

Wealth Checker Canada is for education and planning context only. It is not financial, tax, legal or investment advice.

FAQ

How to use the comparison well

Who is this comparison most useful for?

It is designed for Canadians ages 25-49 who want context for income, debt, housing equity, savings and household finances during high-change years.

Does it compare me with people exactly like me?

No. Public tables use groups, not individual household records. The app uses your age, province and household type to create an approximate, transparent benchmark.

Why compare income and net worth together?

Income describes current earning power. Net worth reflects assets minus debts. Looking at both can provide more useful context when assessing progress over time.

Is this financial advice?

No. It is an educational comparison tool. Use it as one input alongside your own goals and qualified advice where appropriate.
